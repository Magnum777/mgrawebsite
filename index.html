About the MGRA

NOTE: MGRA meets the third Thursday of every month at 7pm at the Warner Robins Church of Christ. If this is your first meeting you can reach out to us on Facebook or Email for more information.

The Middle Georgia Radio Association was formed as a different kind of radio club, one focused on technical and operational radio fundamentals. We offer monthly testing sessions at zero cost every month if you want to earn your amateur radio operator license! Check out our calendar for events, testing, and other engagements.

Our main mission is to provide an educational environment to the public along with ham radio operators throughout the area to learn, experiment, and enjoy the art of amateur radio. We offer technical seminars, public education/exposure, operating events, field day, special events, emergency communication prep, and help with station setups. We are active on the air, on social media, and on email to keep everyone in the know. During the week you will find the MGRA repeaters the most active with technical discussions in Middle Georgia and provide an easy link to reaching out to us.

We hope that you can find a place with us — to nurture the hobby of radio communication so that it may be enjoyed and thrive for many more generations to enjoy.

📝 Testing Sessions

MGRA provides Amateur Radio license testing at no cost every month. Test sessions are held on the second Saturday at the Church of Christ (1947 Watson Blvd., Warner Robins) at 9:00am.

You must register with us by email with your name and FRN to ensure seats are available and the session isn't moved or cancelled.

Note: MGRA does not charge any fee to test, however the FCC requires a $35 payment to process a passing score once your test has been processed by the FCC. This fee is collected by the FCC and IS NOT collected by MGRA.

AI & Digital Modes

How AI interfaces with ham radio and getting your FT-891 on digital

🤖 AI + Ham Radio = The Future

AI is already transforming amateur radio in ways most operators don't realize. From automated propagation prediction to intelligent logging and real-time band analysis, machine learning is making contacts smarter.

What AI can do for your station:

  • 📡 Predict best bands before you transmit
  • 🎯 Decode weak signals humans miss (FT8/JS8Call)
  • 🗺️ Auto-log QSOs with grid squares & signal reports
  • 📊 Analyze your success patterns by band/mode/time
  • 🤖 Auto-respond to CQ on digital modes
  • 📈 Suggest antenna adjustments for better SWR

The MGRA AI Lab: We're experimenting with AI-assisted operating. Members get access to propagation forecasts tuned to Middle Georgia, automated spot reporting, and digital mode coaching.

📻 Getting Your FT-891 on Digital

Your Yaesu FT-891 is perfectly capable of FT8, JS8Call, PSK31, and more. Here's the exact setup:

  1. Download & Install Drivers

    Get the Silicon Labs CP210x drivers for the FT-891's built-in USB CAT control.

    Install WSJT-X for FT8/FT4 or JS8Call for keyboard-to-keyboard.

  2. Connect USB Cable

    Use a USB A-to-B printer cable to the back panel USB port. NOT the 8-pin Mini-DIN — that's for audio only.

    Check Device Manager for two new COM ports. Note the higher number for CAT control.

  3. Radio Menu Settings

    MENU > 05-06 CAT RATE → Set to 4800 baud (or higher, but match in software)

    MENU > 05-07 CAT RTS → Set to ENABLE for PTT via CAT

    MENU > 08-01 DATA MODE → Set to OTHERS (not PSK)

    MENU > 08-03 OTHER DISP → Set to 1500 Hz

  4. WSJT-X Configuration

    Go to File > Settings > Radio:

    • Rig: Yaesu FT-891

    • CAT Port: COM X (higher COM number)

    • Baud: Match radio (4800 or higher)

    • PTT: CAT (not RTS)

    • Audio: USB Audio CODEC (from rear panel)

  5. Audio Levels & First Contact

    Set DATA GAIN knob to about 9 o'clock. Adjust in Windows sound settings until waterfall shows clear signals.

    Enable "DX Call" in WSJT-X, double-click a CQ, hit Enable Tx. Your FT-891 is now working FT8!

💡 MGRA Pro Tip: Our club has a Digimode-4 interface and spare SignaLink for members to borrow. Email us to check one out for Field Day or your first digital experiments.
